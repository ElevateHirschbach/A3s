(A) Background
Before the VP of Process Improvement role was established, Hirschbach had begun work on improving Cold Chain Compliance. Leadership then redirected the new VP's first major initiative to focus on reducing cargo claims. In 2024, Hirschbach recorded 467 cargo claims totaling $5.4M, with $3.2M (59%) identified as preventable. Note: The A3 for this initiative was presented to the ELC on 2/20/2025.
(B) Current State
📊 Claim Breakdown — $5.4M Total (467 claims)
• Temperature Abuse: $1.7M (32%) — 150 claims
  – $926K Solutions · $644K OTR · $171K Local · $9K DED
  – $1M from Walmart, Tyson, & Kroger alone
• Crashes: $1.2M (23%) — 22 claims
• Mechanical Failures: $741K (14%) — 11 claims
• Damage/Theft/Shift/Seals: $713K (13%) — 98 claims
  – 18 broken seal events = $251K in losses
  – Seal guard 55% fail rate at East Dubuque
• Trailer Fires: $470K (9%) — 10 events
  – 12 fires linked to low tire pressure
• OS&D / Late Loads: $413K (8%) — 25 claims
🌡️ Temperature Management Crisis
• 75% of reefer loads run in manual override
• 54 preset variations — too many to select accurately
• Only 17% of EDI orders contain usable temp setpoints
• AS400→Mastermind blank temp = converted to 0°F (false data)
• Customers often don't tender temp, mode, commodity, or value of freight
📋 QA Process Gaps
• QA manually reviews every image for every load
• Hours of delay between L-Call and QA review
• No exception-based reporting; works from spreadsheets
• Reefer alarms, fuel issues, wrong settings go undetected
⚠️ Operational Process Gaps
• No standard SOP for loaded call, empty call, or T-call
• "Us vs. them" mentality between Dubuque & Sapulpa
• Eliminated JCT processes were sometimes better
• Manual empty call process causes significant delays
👥 Roles & Responsibilities Confusion
• QA owns all temp issues; OS&D handles rejected freight
• CSRs, DLs overlook tasks — assume QA will catch it later
• No OS&D support for Solutions business segment
• QA sends emails when phone calls are needed
🚛 Driver & Training Issues
• Incorrect reefer settings by drivers
• Delayed loaded or empty calls
• Inaccurate photos during loaded call
• Confusion on continuous vs. start-stop settings
🔧 Maintenance & Reporting
• 17% of reefer services overdue
• Road service only responds to "act now" / shutdown alerts
• Too many false alarm codes undermine urgency
• Telematics not integrated into AS400
📍 Geo Fence & EDI
• Duplicate geofence locations → inaccurate reporting
• Only 50 of EDI customers onboarded; top 5 not yet included
• Cannot automate arrival/departure without clean geo data
(C) Problem Statement
Hirschbach's cargo claims rate is 1.44 per 1,000 orders (preventable: 0.61), driven by temperature abuse, unclear ownership, manual processes, and fragmented systems. $3.2M in preventable losses annually erodes margins, damages customer relationships, and exposes the company to escalating risk.
(D) Root Cause Analysis
🌡️ Temperature Management
• Confusion around "correct" temp source
• BOL is source of truth — but often missing/unreadable
• EDI platform captures only 17% of temps
• Continuous vs. start-stop confusion
• 54 presets overwhelm drivers → 75% override
📋 Process & Ownership
• Undefined SOPs for loaded/empty/T-calls
• Unclear who enters temps in AS400
• OS&D issues communicated via text — invisible
• Manual empty call creates delays and gaps
• No defined escalation process
💻 Systems & Reporting
• No exception-based reporting or dashboard
• AS400 blank → Mastermind converts to 0°F
• Telematics not integrated with dispatch
• Geofence tables have duplicate locations
• Heavy manual/admin tasks for QA
• No stop gap at empty call to ensure freight is not left in trailer
🎓 Driver Training
• Incorrect reefer settings by drivers
• Delayed loaded or empty calls
• Inaccurate photos submitted during loaded call
• Continuous vs. start-stop temperature settings
• What to do when encounter OS&D related issues
📊 Reporting
• Lack of proactive management due to manual tasks required to identify issues
• Absence of exception reporting
• Heavy administrative and manual tasks
• Inefficient communication methods (email vs. phone), further hinder effective management
🔧 Maintenance & Hardware
• Overdue reefer services (17%)
• Ineffective response to preventative alerts — not urgent until "shut down" or "act now" alert is received
• Fuel alerts received for low fuel, load looks to be empty
(E) Goal
Preventable Cargo Claims per 1,000 Orders: 0.61 → 0.30 (goal)  📅 Target: March 31, 2026
(F) Proposed Countermeasures
• Streamline QA workflows to catch shipment and equipment issues earlier.
• Tighten OS&D processes to improve communication and collaboration between operations and customers.
• Automate e‑call checks to verify load details and prevent product from remaining on trailers.
• Migrate all customers from the legacy EDI system to the Orderful platform to ensure accurate capture of temperature setpoints.
• Clean and standardize geofence tables to remove duplicates, improve reporting accuracy, and support automated arrival/departure events.
• Fix the AS400-to-Mastermind issue where blank temperature fields are converted to zero instead of remaining empty.
• Build a Cargo Variance Dashboard to give maintenance, operations, and QA real‑time visibility into reefer setpoints and temperature exceptions.
• Establish unique customer codes by enforcing deduplication rules and standardizing the code creation process.
• Advance the Intelliset program with updated training and technology to clearly distinguish new vs. legacy settings.
• Implement a tiered loaded‑trailer escalation process (T1/T2/T3) based on load value, freight sensitivity, seasonal risk, and customer requirements.
• Automate L‑call processes so customer temperature/mode requirements flow directly and accurately to the reefer unit.
