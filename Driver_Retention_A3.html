Hirschbach driver turnover has reached 144% (13-week average), prompting an urgent need for process improvement. At Richard's request, we're launching an A3 to identify and implement high-impact, low-effort countermeasures within a 3–6-month timeline.

(C) Problem
As of April 4, 2025, driver turnover is at a at 144% (13-week average), far exceeding the 110% target, resulting in ~$28M annual cost, degraded safety performance, inconsistent service quality, and increased operational strain.
(D) ROOT CAUSE
🏠 Home Time & Work-Life Balance Breakdowns
• Inconsistent or missed home time commitments
• Drivers contacted during home time
• Home-time confusion due to geofence-based delivery proximity
• Lack of visibility into or planning for driver home time preferences
• Inconsistent Home time; ideally, it should occur every 14 to 21 days
❓ Support, Ownership & Communication Gaps
• No centralized owner of Driver Experience across functions
• Support after onboarding is weak or nonexistent
• Phone queues lack SOPs (size, urgency, response time)
• Routing drivers to random Driver Leaders weakens the connection with their assigned leader
• Driver Leaders lack time, trust-building, follow-through
• Driver tools (e.g., driver app) aren't designed with drivers in mind
• Orientation fails to cover key real-world topics in depth
📋 Onboarding & Driver Development
• Early days (first 90–180) lack structure and reinforcement
• No structured mentorship or peer-guidance program
• Driver Development team lacks visibility, empowerment, and accountability
– Team name/branding doesn't clearly communicate purpose or value
• No consistent system for tracking driver feedback, issues, or trends
👥 Cultural Disconnect & Trust Erosion
• Lack of fairness, respect, and trust drives attrition
• Communication is inconsistent, unclear, or constantly shifting
• Drivers feel dismissed or "talked down to"—not treated as part of a team
• Lack of transparency and follow-through erodes confidence in leadership
• Driver Leaders often lack practical understanding of equipment and on-the-road experience
